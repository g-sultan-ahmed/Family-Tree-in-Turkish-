Memory Tips

How to remember

🔑
Age defines siblings

Turkish has separate words for older vs younger siblings. Ağabey / Abi = older brother, Abla = older sister. Younger ones are just kardeş with a gender prefix.

🌿
Anneanne = Anne's Anne

The word literally builds itself. Anne = mother, so Anneanne = "mother's mother." Same logic: Babaanne = father's mother (from Baba).

💬
The -m suffix means "my"

Add -m or -ım to make it possessive. Anne → Annem (my mother). Oğul → Oğlum (my son). You'll hear this constantly in everyday Turkish.

🌳
Büyük = Big / Great

Büyük means big or great. So Büyükanne = "great mother" (grandmother) and Büyükbaba = "great father" (grandfather). Poetic and logical!

🎯
Dede is universal

Dede is the warmest, most common word for grandfather — like saying "grandpa." It works for both maternal and paternal grandfathers in everyday life.

👶
Torun completes the cycle

Your grandchildren call you Büyükanne or Büyükbaba — the very same words you used for your own grandparents. The family tree beautifully comes full circle.
