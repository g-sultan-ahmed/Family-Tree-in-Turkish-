Memory Tips

How to remember

🔑
Age defines siblings

Turkish has separate words for older vs younger siblings. Ağabey / Abi = older brother, Abla = older sister. Younger ones are just kardeş with a gender prefix.

🌿
Anneanne = Anne's Anne

The word builds itself! Anne means mother, so Anneanne is literally "mother's mother." Same logic: Babaanne is from Baba (father).

💬
-m suffix = "my"

Add -m or -ım to make it possessive. Anne → Annem (my mother). Oğul → Oğlum (my son). Very common in everyday speech.

🌳
Büyük = Big

Büyük means big/great in Turkish. So Büyükanne = "big mother" (grandmother) and Büyükbaba = "big father" (grandfather). Poetic and logical!

🎯
Dede is just Dede

Dede is the most common word for grandfather — warm and familiar, like "grandpa." It's used by children and adults alike, often for both maternal and paternal grandfathers.

👶
Torun completes the circle

Your grandchildren call you Büyükanne or Büyükbaba — the same words you used for your own grandparents. The family tree truly cycles.
